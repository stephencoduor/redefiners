Grammar Guide: Verb Conjugations

Last edited Mar 18, 2026 by Prof. Maria Garcia

Introduction to Spanish Verb Conjugations

Understanding verb conjugations is one of the most important skills you will develop in your study of Spanish. Unlike English, Spanish verbs change their endings to indicate who is performing the action and when it occurs. This guide covers the essential conjugation patterns for the present tense.

Regular Verb Groups

Spanish verbs are divided into three groups based on their infinitive endings:

  • -AR verbs (hablar, caminar, estudiar) — the largest group
  • -ER verbs (comer, beber, leer)
  • -IR verbs (vivir, escribir, abrir)

Present Tense: -AR Verbs (hablar — to speak)

Pronoun Ending Conjugation Translation
yo-ohabloI speak
tú-ashablasyou speak
él/ella/usted-ahablahe/she speaks
nosotros-amoshablamoswe speak
vosotros-áishabláisyou all speak
ellos/ustedes-anhablanthey speak

Present Tense: -ER Verbs (comer — to eat)

Pronoun Ending Conjugation Translation
yo-ocomoI eat
tú-escomesyou eat
él/ella/usted-ecomehe/she eats
nosotros-emoscomemoswe eat
vosotros-éiscoméisyou all eat
ellos/ustedes-encomenthey eat

Common Irregular Verbs

Some frequently used verbs do not follow regular conjugation patterns. The most important irregular verbs to memorize include:

  1. ser (to be - permanent): soy, eres, es, somos, sois, son
  2. estar (to be - temporary): estoy, estás, está, estamos, estáis, están
  3. ir (to go): voy, vas, va, vamos, vais, van
  4. tener (to have): tengo, tienes, tiene, tenemos, tenéis, tienen
  5. hacer (to do/make): hago, haces, hace, hacemos, hacéis, hacen

Practice Tips

To master verb conjugations, try these strategies:

  • Create flashcards for each verb group and review daily
  • Practice writing sentences using newly learned conjugations
  • Use the online conjugation practice tool linked in Module 2
  • Listen to the audio exercises to hear correct pronunciation

Remember: consistent daily practice of 15–20 minutes is more effective than occasional long study sessions. Refer to the Vocabulary List for additional context on using these verbs in sentences.
