Abstract

This paper examines the ethical implications of genetic engineering technologies, including CRISPR-Cas9 gene editing, with a focus on therapeutic applications versus enhancement purposes. The analysis draws on bioethical frameworks including principlism, virtue ethics, and consequentialism to evaluate current practices and future possibilities in human genetic modification.

Introduction

The rapid advancement of genetic engineering technologies has opened unprecedented possibilities for treating hereditary diseases, improving crop yields, and fundamentally altering biological organisms. Since the discovery of CRISPR-Cas9 as a programmable gene-editing tool, the scientific community has grappled with profound ethical questions regarding the boundaries of acceptable genetic modification...
