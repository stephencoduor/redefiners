Course Description

This intermediate Spanish course builds on the foundations established in Spanish 101. Students will develop conversational fluency through guided dialogues, role-playing scenarios, and cultural immersion activities. The course emphasizes practical communication skills for real-world situations including travel, dining, healthcare, and professional settings. Students will also deepen their understanding of Spanish-speaking cultures across Latin America and Spain.

Learning Objectives

Engage in sustained conversations on everyday topics using intermediate-level vocabulary and grammar structures
Conjugate and correctly use the subjunctive mood in common conversational contexts
Demonstrate comprehension of native-speed audio and video materials on familiar topics
Write structured paragraphs and short essays using proper grammar and cohesive transitions
Compare and contrast cultural practices across Spanish-speaking regions
