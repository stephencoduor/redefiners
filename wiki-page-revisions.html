1 # Introduction to Cell Biology
2
3 ## Overview
4 Cells are the basic unit of life. All living organisms are composed of cells.
4 Cells are the fundamental structural and functional units of all living organisms. Every living thing, from single-celled bacteria to complex multicellular humans, is composed of cells.
5
6 ## Cell Types
7 There are two main types of cells:
8 - **Prokaryotic cells** - lack a membrane-bound nucleus
9 - **Eukaryotic cells** - have a nucleus
9 - **Eukaryotic cells** - contain a well-defined, membrane-bound nucleus and organelles
10
11 ## Cell Theory
12 The modern cell theory includes three key principles:
13 1. All living organisms are composed of one or more cells
14 2. The cell is the basic unit of structure and organization
15 3. All cells arise from pre-existing cells
16
17 ## Key Organelles
18 The nucleus contains DNA and controls cell activity.
18 The **nucleus** houses the cell's genetic material (DNA) and serves as the control center for cell growth, metabolism, and reproduction.
19 The **mitochondria** are responsible for cellular respiration and ATP production.
