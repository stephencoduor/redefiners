Spanish 101: Introduction to Spanish

Spring 2026  •  Section A  •  MWF 10:00 – 10:50 AM

Course Description

This introductory Spanish course is designed for students with little or no prior knowledge of the language. Students will develop foundational skills in listening, speaking, reading, and writing while exploring the diverse cultures of the Spanish-speaking world. By the end of the semester, students will be able to engage in basic conversations, understand simple texts, and express ideas in written form.

Required Materials

  • Descubre: Lengua y cultura del mundo hispánico, Level 1, 4th Edition
  • Access to the online learning platform (included with textbook)
  • A Spanish-English dictionary (physical or digital)
  • Notebook for in-class exercises and notes

Grading Policy

  • Participation & Attendance: 15%
  • Homework & Online Exercises: 20%
  • Quizzes (4 total): 20%
  • Midterm Exam: 15%
  • Oral Presentation: 10%
  • Final Exam: 20%

Attendance Policy

Regular attendance is essential in a language course. Each unexcused absence beyond two will result in a 2% deduction from your participation grade. Arriving more than 10 minutes late counts as an absence. Please notify the instructor in advance if you will miss class for an excused reason.

Academic Integrity

All work submitted must be your own. Use of translation software (e.g., Google Translate) for assignments is not permitted. Collaboration on homework is encouraged for study purposes, but each student must submit their own original work. Violations will be referred to the Office of Academic Affairs.
