Can you explain the esterification mechanism for aspirin synthesis? I have a lab report due on this topic.

Great question! The synthesis of aspirin involves an esterification reaction between salicylic acid and acetic anhydride. Here is the step-by-step mechanism:

Step 1: Nucleophilic Attack
The hydroxyl group (-OH) on salicylic acid acts as a nucleophile, attacking the electrophilic carbonyl carbon of acetic anhydride.

Step 2: Tetrahedral Intermediate
A tetrahedral intermediate forms as the carbonyl bond breaks to accommodate the new bond.

Step 3: Elimination
Acetic acid is eliminated as a leaving group, and the ester bond (C-O-C) forms, yielding acetylsalicylic acid (aspirin).

Key Points for Your Lab Report:

  • Phosphoric acid is commonly used as a catalyst
  • The reaction is typically conducted at 85 degrees Celsius
  • Recrystallization is used to purify the product
