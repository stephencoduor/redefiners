Cultural Traditions of Latin America: A Comparative Study of Day of the Dead Celebrations

Sofia Rodriguez | Spanish 201 | March 21, 2026

The Day of the Dead, known as el Dia de los Muertos, represents one of the most significant cultural traditions across Latin America. While commonly associated with Mexico, this celebration manifests in diverse forms throughout the region, reflecting unique historical, indigenous, and colonial influences that have shaped each community's relationship with remembrance and mortality.

In the central highlands of Mexico, particularly in the state of Michoacan, the celebration takes on an especially elaborate character. Families gather in cemeteries to construct intricate ofrendas adorned with cempasuchil (marigold) flowers, pan de muerto, and cherished belongings of the deceased.
