Week 5: Subjunctive Mood - When do you use it?

Prof. Martinez
· Posted Mar 18, 2026 · 18 replies

This week we are diving into one of the most challenging aspects of Spanish grammar: the subjunctive mood. Many English speakers find this concept difficult because English rarely uses a distinct subjunctive form.

The subjunctive is used in Spanish to express wishes, doubts, emotions, recommendations, and hypothetical situations. For example, compare these two sentences:

  • Indicative: "Ella habla espanol." (She speaks Spanish - a fact)
  • Subjunctive: "Espero que ella hable espanol." (I hope she speaks Spanish - a wish/desire)

For this discussion, please share at least two original sentences using the subjunctive mood. Explain why the subjunctive is required in each case. Then, respond to at least one classmate's sentences with feedback or questions.

Excellent question, Ana! Yes, that is exactly correct. When you negate "creer" (to believe), you introduce doubt, which triggers the subjunctive. Compare: "Creo que es verdad" (indicative) vs. "No creo que sea verdad" (subjunctive). Great observation!
